Caltrans’ California Integrated Travel Project (Cal-ITP) is helping modernize fare collection and trip planning systems to make travel planning and fare payment simpler and more cost-effective for public transportation providers and riders in California and beyond. After you complete our Cal-ITP Support Request Form, we welcome you to browse this page to learn more about modernizing your payment systems.

Reach out to hello@calitp.org for support at any time as your agency explores these resources.

Contactless payments

California is making it easier for public transit providers to accept contactless credit/debit/prepaid cards or mobile wallets on a smart device through Master Service Agreements (MSAs) established by the California Department of General Services (DGS). Transit providers can take advantage of the MSAs to purchase the key hardware and software needed to accept contactless payments. (See the MSAs on our Contracts page.)

The Contactless Payments MSA Information Packet (PDF) provides an overview of MSA-related information so you can familiarize yourself with the contract, and what steps your agency needs to take to move forward with buying from the MSAs.

If you’re looking for an informational presentation on the MSAs for agency staff or your board of directors, this PDF is in slideshow format to meet those needs.

What else do I need to do to understand how contactless payments work?

After you’ve read through the Contactless Payments MSA Information Packet, move onto this Contactless Payments Introduction (PDF). Geared toward transit agency staff, it contains:

  • an overview of the different components of a contactless payment system
  • information on how contactless payment transactions work
  • an explanation of what it means to become a merchant

If you’re looking for an informational presentation on contactless payments for agency staff or your board of directors, this PDF is in slideshow format to meet those needs.

I’m ready to move forward—how do I draft a Scope of Work?

After you’ve read through the Contactless Payments MSA Information Packet and Contactless Payments Introduction, move onto this MSA SOW Template and Guidance (PDF).

This PDF provides detailed guidance for transit agency staff on how to complete a User Agreement Scope of Work as required to participate in the Master Service Agreements (MSAs) for Payment Acceptance Devices (PADs) and Transit Processor Services. Completing it will assist your agency with key scoping decisions as you work toward purchasing hardware and software services.

How much will this cost to implement?

When you’re ready to start creating cost estimates for purchasing contactless payment hardware and software services, this Excel Workbook will help transit agency staff to a) compare vendor prices and develop ballpark estimates for budgeting purposes (and board presentations), and b) make scoping and vendor decisions during the vendor engagement phase.
